Conclusion

When it comes to spending on education in the ten selected countries, we can see that both the United States and China rank among the top. This is closely related to the relatively developed GDP of these two countries.

In the bubble chart of GDP per capita and education expenditure per capita, we can see how much each individual citizen values education. China, for example, has a low GDP per capita but high spending on private education.

Education Expenditure Growth (%)

In both combo charts, it is easier to see the trend of the growth rate on the chart and to compare it with other countries. Take China as an example. From 2010 to 2016, the growth trend of China's education expenditure was small increase and decrease. So we can predict a downward trend in China's education spending over the next few years.
